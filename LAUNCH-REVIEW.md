# RielArt.com Final Launch Review

Reviewed: July 13, 2026

## Overall status

The public website is ready for launch as a professional first version. Its positioning, navigation, service structure, pricing, process, portal connection, inquiry paths, legal baseline, and content architecture are consistent.

This update intentionally avoids invented client results or unverified team claims. Representative work is labeled as representative, and the About page now presents RielArt as a founder-led studio with specialist collaborators added only when needed.

## Sitewide technical polish

- Internal RielArt links open in the same browser tab.
- The RielArt Client Portal also opens in the same tab.
- External sources, LinkedIn, and Stripe checkout links open in a new tab with safe `noopener noreferrer` attributes.
- All local page, image, stylesheet, and script paths were checked and resolve correctly.
- No duplicate HTML IDs were found.
- All images have an `alt` attribute.
- Every main indexed page includes a title, description, canonical URL, and one clear H1.
- Existing sitemap and robots files remain valid for the public RielArt site. The portal is a separate application and is not added to the public marketing sitemap.


## Homepage visual refinements — v12

- Replaced the three large hero connector cards with clean standalone Brand, Website, and AI icons.
- Removed the numbered circles from the four delivery cards and gave each point more balanced space.
- Rebuilt the Client Portal panel in the Delivery & Visibility section with a responsive card layout, clearer capabilities, and a stronger portal call to action.

## Page-by-page review

### Home — Ready

- Clear primary positioning across brand, website, and practical AI systems.
- Hero visual now introduces the **RielArt Portal** rather than a vague internal system.
- Added a stronger outcomes section based on defined scope, visible milestones, documented handoff, and maintainable delivery.
- Added a dedicated RielArt Client Portal explanation covering projects, updates, support, and passwordless access.
- Portal billing is accurately described as being in development while payments remain handled through Stripe.

### Services — Ready

- Service areas are distinct and easy to compare.
- Ongoing management is separated from one-time project work.
- Added a client-delivery callout explaining how active work is connected through the RielArt Client Portal.

### Pricing — Ready

- Fixed projects, custom systems, and monthly subscriptions are separated clearly.
- Currency, taxes, third-party costs, timelines, scope, revisions, and recurring billing expectations are visible.
- Added clear separation between Stripe billing and the RielArt Client Portal.
- Stripe remains the source for checkout, payment, subscription, and billing records while portal billing tools are developed.

### Process — Ready

- Four-stage process is understandable from discovery through launch and handoff.
- Client responsibilities and dependency risks are stated.
- Added a portal delivery section explaining where clients can follow projects, tasks, dates, updates, and support.

### Work / Portfolio — Ready as an initial portfolio

- Representative solution scenarios are useful and now explicitly identified as scenarios rather than fabricated client case studies.
- Named client work and measured outcomes should be added only after permission and verification.
- This page can launch now and become stronger as real completed work is published.

### About — Ready

- Repositioned as a founder-led studio.
- Removed the unverified named team member.
- Added transparent wording about specialist collaborators.
- Added Client Portal delivery to the maintainability promise.

### Insights — Ready

- The index and nine current articles provide useful subject coverage across web performance, local SEO, brand, AI, automation, and maintenance.
- Internal article navigation now stays in the same tab.
- External reference links retain safe new-tab behavior.

### Contact — Ready

- Message form and booking option are distinct.
- Existing clients now have a dedicated RielArt Client Portal sign-in callout after the main inquiry section.
- New prospects are still directed to the form or call flow.

### Privacy Policy — Ready as a practical launch baseline

- Updated to include website inquiries and RielArt Client Portal data.
- Identifies Cloudflare, Resend, Stripe, Formspree, and Calendly roles.
- Clarifies that full payment-card details are not stored by RielArt.

This is a practical business website baseline, not formal legal advice. A lawyer should review it if RielArt begins handling regulated data, serving additional jurisdictions, hiring employees, or entering larger enterprise contracts.

### Terms & Conditions — Ready as a practical launch baseline

- Covers website information, proposals, project agreements, pricing, client responsibilities, ownership, and outcome limitations.
- Added approved-email access, one-time code security, and portal responsibility terms.
- Clarifies that portal content does not replace signed agreements or Stripe billing records.

### 404, Thank-you, Redirect pages — Ready

- 404 page provides a useful route back into the site.
- Thank-you page confirms inquiry receipt and now offers Client Portal access for existing clients.
- Legacy privacy, terms, and package routes remain handled.

## Best next improvements after launch

1. Replace one representative portfolio scenario with a verified completed project when permission is available.
2. Add a real testimonial only after receiving explicit client approval.
3. Connect portal project records to real client engagements and keep public wording aligned with released portal capabilities.
4. Add analytics and Search Console verification after production deployment, then review actual visitor behavior before making another major design change.

## Launch recommendation

Publish this version and begin using it. The website is past the point where more visual redesign is the highest-value activity. The next gains should come from real inquiries, completed work, verified proof, portal usage, and measured visitor behavior.
